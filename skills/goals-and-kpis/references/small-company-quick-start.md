## Why Small Companies Fail at Goals

| Failure Mode | Why It Happens | Fix |
|--------------|----------------|-----|
| **Too many goals** | Trying to copy Google | Max 3 company OKRs, period |
| **Goals change weekly** | Reacting to every fire | Commit for the quarter, no changes |
| **No one remembers them** | No review cadence | 15-min Monday check-in, non-negotiable |
| **Goals feel fake** | Disconnected from daily work | Every task should map to a KR |
| **Sandbagging** | Fear of missing goals | Make them stretch goals (70% = success) |
| **Vanity metrics** | Measuring activity not impact | Only metrics you'd trade money for |

## KPIs for Small Companies (Keep It Simple)

### Stage 1: Pre-Product-Market Fit (5-15 people)

**Track Only These:**

| Metric | Why | How to Measure |
|--------|-----|----------------|
| **Active users (or customers)** | Are people using it? | Your database |
| **Usage frequency** | Are they coming back? | Analytics tool (Mixpanel, Amplitude) |
| **NPS or CSAT** | Do they love it? | Quarterly survey (use Delighted) |
| **Runway (months)** | Will we survive? | Bank balance / monthly burn |
| **Revenue or MRR** | Can we pay the bills? | Your billing system |

**Don't track:** CAC, LTV, churn cohorts, sales velocity â€” too early.

---

**Add These:**

| Department | Core KPIs (3-5 max) |
|------------|---------------------|

| **Marketing** | MQLs, Cost per Lead, MQLâ†’SQL Conversion |
| **Product** | Feature Adoption, DAU/MAU, Time to Value |
| **Customer Success** | NRR, Churn Rate, NPS |
| **Finance** | Revenue, Burn Rate, Runway, Gross Margin |

**Meeting Cadence:**
- Weekly: Team-level OKR check-ins (30 min)
- Monthly: Department reviews with leadership (60 min)
- Quarterly: All-hands planning session (half-day)

## Common Mistakes Small Companies Make

### Mistake 1: Copying Google's OKR System

**Problem:** Google has 100,000 employees. You have 15.

**Fix:** Use a simplified version:
- No individual OKRs (just company + department)
- No complex scoring systems
- No fancy OKR software

---

### Mistake 2: Setting Too Many Goals

**Problem:** "We need to grow revenue AND build product AND hire AND improve culture AND..."

**Fix:** Ruthlessly prioritize. If everything is a priority, nothing is.

**Rule:** Max 3 company OKRs. If you can only do ONE thing this quarter, what moves the needle most?

---

### Mistake 3: Goals That Don't Drive Behavior

**Problem:** OKR says "Increase NPS to 50" but no one changes what they do.

**Fix:** For each KR, ask: "What will we do DIFFERENTLY to achieve this?"

**Example:**
- âŒ Bad KR: Increase NPS to 50
- âœ… Good KR: Increase NPS to 50 by implementing in-app onboarding + monthly customer interviews

---

### Mistake 4: No Weekly Rhythm

**Problem:** Set goals in January, forgot by February.

**Fix:** 15-minute Monday morning check-in, no exceptions.

**Format:**
- Each owner: % progress, confidence (ðŸŸ¢ðŸŸ¡ðŸ”´), top priority this week
- Identify blockers
- Done

---

### Mistake 5: Sandbag Targets

**Problem:** Setting goals you know you'll hit because you're scared to fail.

**Fix:** OKRs should be stretch goals. 70% achievement = success.

**Example:**
- If you're confident you'll hit 30 customers, set the target at 45
- If you think you can close $500K, aim for $700K

## Graduation Path: When to Add Complexity

| Company Stage | System | Meeting Cadence |
|---------------|--------|-----------------|
| **5-10 people** | Simple OKRs (company-level only) | Weekly 15-min check-in |
| **10-20 people** | OKRs + monthly reviews | Weekly check-ins + monthly review |
| **20-50 people** | OKRs with 2-level cascade | Weekly (teams) + monthly (leadership) |
| **50-100 people** | OKRs + Goal Map (annual) | Weekly + monthly + quarterly |
| **100-250 people** | OKRs + Goal Map + Department dashboards | Full cadence (weekly â†’ annual) |
| **250+ people** | Consider BSC or formal V2MOM | Enterprise review structure |

## Final Advice for Small Company Leaders

1. **Start simple.** You can always add complexity later.
2. **Consistency > perfection.** Better to track 3 metrics weekly than 30 metrics never.
3. **Make it public.** Everyone should see the OKRs and dashboard.
4. **Celebrate progress.** 0.7 on a stretch goal is a win, not a failure.
5. **Kill bad goals fast.** If a goal becomes irrelevant mid-quarter, drop it. Don't be precious.

The best goal system is the one you'll actually use. Start with the minimum viable version and evolve as you grow.
