## Key Takeaways Across All Case Studies

### What Success Looks Like

1. **Start simple** â€” Case 1 (18 people) did Google Sheet + 15-min check-ins
2. **Involve the team** â€” Case 3 failed when top-down, succeeded when co-created
3. **Less is more** â€” All successful cases had 3-5 objectives max
4. **Weekly rhythm matters** â€” All cases emphasized weekly check-ins
5. **First quarter is learning** â€” 0.7-0.8 score is great for Q1

### Red Flags

| Red Flag | What It Means | How to Fix |
|----------|---------------|-----------|
| OKRs never mentioned in meetings | Goals aren't real | Make them part of weekly rituals |
| Scoring 1.0 on everything | Goals too easy | Make them stretch targets |
| Scoring <0.3 on everything | Goals unrealistic or misaligned | Reset mid-quarter if needed |
| Departments hit goals but company fails | Poor cascade | Use cross-functional review process |
| Team doesn't know the OKRs | Communication failure | All-hands updates, visible dashboard |

### When to Pivot

It's okay to change goals mid-quarter if:
- The market shifts dramatically (Case 3: COVID-like event)
- You discover the goal was based on bad data
- A dependency breaks (key hire doesn't happen)
- The goal becomes irrelevant

**Rule:** Don't pivot due to difficulty. Only pivot due to irrelevance.
