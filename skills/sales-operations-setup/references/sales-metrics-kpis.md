# Sales Metrics & KPIs Reference Guide

## Overview
This guide covers essential metrics for monitoring and optimizing sales operations performance.

---

## Pipeline Metrics

### 1. Pipeline Value
**Definition:** Total value of all open opportunities
**Formula:** `Σ (All Open Opportunity Amounts)`
**Target:** 3-5x monthly revenue target
**Frequency:** Daily

**Example:**
- 100 opportunities @ average $50K = $5M pipeline
- Monthly target: $1M
- Coverage ratio: 5x ✅

---

### 2. Weighted Pipeline
**Definition:** Pipeline adjusted for stage probability
**Formula:** `Σ (Opportunity Amount × Stage Probability)`
**Target:** 1.5-2x monthly target
**Frequency:** Weekly

**Example:**
- $5M total pipeline × 40% avg probability = $2M weighted
- Monthly target: $1M
- Weighted coverage: 2x ✅

---

### 3. Pipeline Coverage Ratio
**Definition:** Pipeline value divided by revenue target
**Formula:** `Total Pipeline / Revenue Target`
**Target:** 3-5x for early stages, 1.5x for late stages
**Frequency:** Weekly

**Benchmark:**
- <2x: Critical (insufficient pipeline)
- 2-3x: Warning (below target)
- 3-5x: Healthy
- >5x: Investigate (low conversion or inflated pipeline)

---

### 4. Pipeline Velocity
**Definition:** Speed at which deals move through pipeline
**Formula:** `(# Opportunities × Deal Value × Win Rate) / Sales Cycle Length`
**Target:** Increasing trend
**Frequency:** Monthly

**Example:**
- 20 opportunities × $50K × 25% win rate = $250K
- 60-day sales cycle
- Velocity: $4,166 per day

---

### 5. Pipeline Creation
**Definition:** New pipeline value added in period
**Formula:** `Σ (New Opportunity Amounts in Period)`
**Target:** 1-2x monthly target
**Frequency:** Weekly

**Example:**
- Created $1.5M new pipeline this month
- Monthly target: $1M
- Creation rate: 1.5x ✅

---

## Conversion Metrics

### 6. Lead-to-Opportunity Conversion Rate
**Definition:** % of leads that become opportunities
**Formula:** `(Opportunities Created / Leads Generated) × 100`
**Target:** 10-20% (varies by source)
**Frequency:** Monthly

**Example:**
- 500 leads generated
- 75 opportunities created
- Conversion: 15% ✅

---

### 7. Opportunity-to-Win Rate (Close Rate)
**Definition:** % of opportunities that close as won
**Formula:** `(Deals Won / Total Opportunities) × 100`
**Target:** 20-30% (varies by sales model)
**Frequency:** Monthly

**Example:**
- 100 opportunities created
- 25 deals won
- Win rate: 25% ✅

---

### 8. Stage Conversion Rates
**Definition:** % of opportunities advancing from each stage
**Formula:** `(Advanced to Next Stage / Total in Stage) × 100`
**Target:** Varies by stage
**Frequency:** Monthly

**Example Funnel:**
- Discovery → Demo: 50% (50 of 100)
- Demo → Proposal: 60% (30 of 50)
- Proposal → Negotiation: 70% (21 of 30)
- Negotiation → Won: 80% (17 of 21)
- Overall win rate: 17% ✅

---

### 9. Demo-to-Close Rate
**Definition:** % of demos that result in closed deals
**Formula:** `(Deals Won / Demos Conducted) × 100`
**Target:** 30-40%
**Frequency:** Monthly

**Example:**
- 50 demos conducted
- 18 deals closed
- Demo close rate: 36% ✅

---

## Efficiency Metrics

### 10. Average Sales Cycle Length
**Definition:** Average days from opportunity to close
**Formula:** `Σ (Days to Close for Each Deal) / # Deals`
**Target:** Decreasing trend, <90 days for SMB, <180 for Enterprise
**Frequency:** Monthly

**Example:**
- 20 deals closed
- Total days: 1,400
- Average cycle: 70 days ✅

---

### 11. Average Deal Size (ACV/ACV)
**Definition:** Average contract value
**Formula:** `Total Revenue / # Deals Won`
**Target:** Increasing trend (moving upmarket)
**Frequency:** Monthly

**Example:**
- $500K revenue
- 10 deals closed
- Average deal: $50K

**ACV vs. TCV:**
- ACV (Annual Contract Value): First-year value
- TCV (Total Contract Value): Multi-year total

---

### 12. Sales Cycle by Stage
**Definition:** Average time spent in each stage
**Formula:** `Σ (Days in Stage) / # Opportunities`
**Target:** Track trends, identify bottlenecks
**Frequency:** Monthly

**Example:**
| Stage | Avg Days | Target | Status |
|-------|----------|--------|--------|
| Discovery | 14 | 14 | ✅ |
| Demo | 21 | 14 | ⚠️ Slow |
| Proposal | 10 | 7 | ⚠️ Slow |
| Negotiation | 18 | 21 | ✅ |

**Action:** Focus on speeding up Demo and Proposal stages

---

### 13. Win Rate by Source
**Definition:** Close rate by lead source
**Formula:** `(Deals Won / Opps Created) × 100` per source
**Target:** Optimize spend toward high-converting sources
**Frequency:** Quarterly

**Example:**
| Source | Opps | Wins | Win Rate | Focus |
|--------|------|------|----------|-------|
| Referral | 20 | 10 | 50% | ✅ Double down |
| Inbound | 50 | 15 | 30% | ✅ Good |
| Outbound | 100 | 15 | 15% | ⚠️ Improve |
| Events | 30 | 3 | 10% | ⚠️ Reconsider |

---

## Activity Metrics

### 14. Activities per Rep
**Definition:** Sales activities logged by reps
**Formula:** `Σ (Calls + Emails + Meetings) / # Reps`
**Target:** 50-80 activities per rep per day
**Frequency:** Daily

**Example:**
| Activity | Per Rep/Day | Target | Status |
|----------|-------------|--------|--------|
| Calls | 30 | 40 | ⚠️ Below |
| Emails | 25 | 30 | ⚠️ Below |
| Meetings | 5 | 5 | ✅ |
| Total | 60 | 75 | ⚠️ Below |

---

### 15. Meetings Booked
**Definition:** # of discovery/demo meetings scheduled
**Formula:** `Σ (Meetings Booked in Period)`
**Target:** 5-10 per rep per week
**Frequency:** Weekly

**Example:**
- 10 reps × 7 meetings/week = 70 meetings ✅

---

### 16. Response Rate
**Definition:** % of outreach that gets response
**Formula:** `(Responses / Outreach Attempts) × 100`
**Target:** 10-20% for cold outreach
**Frequency:** Weekly

**Example:**
- 500 emails sent
- 75 responses
- Response rate: 15% ✅

---

## Revenue Metrics

### 17. Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR)
**Definition:** Predictable monthly revenue (SaaS)
**Formula:** `Σ (Monthly Subscription Values)`
**Target:** Increasing month-over-month
**Frequency:** Monthly

**Components:**
- New MRR: From new customers
- Expansion MRR: Upsells/cross-sells
- Contraction MRR: Downgrades
- Churned MRR: Lost customers
- **Net New MRR** = New + Expansion - Contraction - Churned

---

### 18. Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR)
**Definition:** Annualized value of subscriptions
**Formula:** `MRR × 12` or `Σ (Annual Contract Values)`
**Target:** Growth aligned to company goals
**Frequency:** Monthly

**Example:**
- $100K MRR × 12 = $1.2M ARR

---

### 19. Revenue Growth Rate
**Definition:** Period-over-period revenue growth
**Formula:** `((Current Period - Prior Period) / Prior Period) × 100`
**Target:** Varies (early stage 20%+ MoM, mature 20-40% YoY)
**Frequency:** Monthly

**Example:**
- Last month: $800K
- This month: $1M
- Growth: 25% ✅

---

### 20. Quota Attainment
**Definition:** % of reps hitting quota
**Formula:** `(# Reps at ≥100% of Quota / Total Reps) × 100`
**Target:** 60-80% of reps
**Frequency:** Monthly

**Example:**
- 20 total reps
- 14 at ≥100% quota
- Attainment: 70% ✅

**Benchmark:**
- <50%: Quota too high or coaching issues
- 50-60%: Below target
- 60-80%: Healthy
- >80%: Quota may be too low

---

## Customer Acquisition Metrics

### 21. Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
**Definition:** Cost to acquire a customer
**Formula:** `(Sales + Marketing Costs) / # New Customers`
**Target:** <1/3 of LTV (3:1 LTV:CAC ratio)
**Frequency:** Monthly

**Example:**
- Sales costs: $50K
- Marketing costs: $30K
- New customers: 20
- CAC: $4K

---

### 22. CAC Payback Period
**Definition:** Months to recover acquisition cost
**Formula:** `CAC / (MRR × Gross Margin %)`
**Target:** <12 months
**Frequency:** Quarterly

**Example:**
- CAC: $4K
- Monthly revenue per customer: $500
- Gross margin: 80%
- Payback: $4K / ($500 × 0.8) = 10 months ✅

---

### 23. Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)
**Definition:** Total revenue from customer over lifetime
**Formula:** `(Avg Revenue per Customer / Churn Rate) × Gross Margin`
**Target:** 3:1 LTV:CAC ratio minimum
**Frequency:** Quarterly

**Example:**
- Average customer: $500/mo
- Churn rate: 5% monthly (20 month lifetime)
- Lifetime revenue: $500 × 20 = $10K
- Gross margin: 80%
- LTV: $10K × 0.8 = $8K
- LTV:CAC: $8K / $4K = 2:1 ⚠️ (below 3:1 target)

---

### 24. Magic Number
**Definition:** Sales efficiency metric
**Formula:** `(Quarterly Revenue Growth × 4) / Prior Quarter S&M Spend`
**Target:** >0.75 (efficient growth)
**Frequency:** Quarterly

**Example:**
- Q1 revenue: $1M
- Q2 revenue: $1.2M
- Growth: $200K
- Q1 S&M spend: $300K
- Magic number: ($200K × 4) / $300K = 2.67 ✅

**Interpretation:**
- <0.5: Inefficient, slow growth
- 0.5-0.75: Approaching efficiency
- 0.75-1.0: Efficient growth
- >1.0: Highly efficient, consider accelerating investment

## Performance Metrics

### 25. Sales Rep Productivity
**Definition:** Revenue per sales rep
**Formula:** `Total Revenue / # Reps`
**Target:** Varies by role (SDR vs. AE vs. Enterprise)
**Frequency:** Monthly

**Example:**
- $1M revenue
- 10 Account Executives
- Productivity: $100K per AE

**Benchmark:**
- SMB AE: $50-100K/month
- Mid-Market AE: $75-150K/month
- Enterprise AE: $100-300K/month

---

### 26. Ramp Time
**Definition:** Time for new rep to reach full productivity
**Formula:** `Days from Start Date to First Month at 100% Quota`
**Target:** 3-6 months
**Frequency:** Per hire

**Example:**
- New hire: Jan 1
- First full quota month: Apr 1
- Ramp time: 3 months ✅

**Benchmark:**
- SDR: 1-2 months
- SMB AE: 3-4 months
- Mid-Market AE: 4-6 months
- Enterprise AE: 6-9 months

---

### 27. Sales Team Retention
**Definition:** % of sales team retained
**Formula:** `(Team Size End of Period / Team Size Start) × 100`
**Target:** >85% annually
**Frequency:** Quarterly

**Example:**
- Started year: 20 reps
- End of year: 18 reps (2 left)
- Retention: 90% ✅

---

### 28. Time to First Deal
**Definition:** Days from hire to first closed deal
**Formula:** `Days from Start Date to First Won Opportunity`
**Target:** <90 days
**Frequency:** Per hire

**Example:**
- New hire: Jan 1
- First deal: Mar 15
- Time to first deal: 74 days ✅

---

## Forecasting Metrics

### 29. Forecast Accuracy
**Definition:** How close forecast was to actual
**Formula:** `(Actual Revenue / Forecasted Revenue) × 100`
**Target:** 95-105%
**Frequency:** Monthly

**Example:**
- Forecast: $1M
- Actual: $1.05M
- Accuracy: 105% ✅

**Trend:**
Track accuracy by category (commit vs. best case)

---

### 30. Slippage Rate
**Definition:** % of forecasted deals that pushed to next period
**Formula:** `(Deals that Slipped / Total Forecasted Deals) × 100`
**Target:** <10%
**Frequency:** Monthly

**Example:**
- Forecasted 20 deals to close
- 3 pushed to next month
- Slippage: 15% ⚠️

---

## Competitive Metrics

### 31. Win Rate vs. Competitor
**Definition:** Win % in competitive deals
**Formula:** `(Wins Against Competitor X / Total Competitive Deals) × 100`
**Target:** >50%
**Frequency:** Quarterly

**Example:**
| Competitor | Deals | Wins | Win Rate | Trend |
|------------|-------|------|----------|-------|
| Competitor A | 20 | 12 | 60% | ✅ |
| Competitor B | 15 | 6 | 40% | ⚠️ |
| Competitor C | 10 | 8 | 80% | ✅ |

---

### 32. Competitive Displacement
**Definition:** % of wins that displaced competitor
**Formula:** `(Wins from Competitors / Total Wins) × 100`
**Target:** Varies by market maturity
**Frequency:** Quarterly

**Example:**
- 30 wins total
- 12 displaced competitor
- Displacement rate: 40%

## Common Mistakes

1. **Too Many Metrics**: Focus on 5-10 key metrics
2. **Vanity Metrics**: Track metrics that drive action
3. **No Benchmarks**: Compare to targets and industry standards
4. **Lack of Trends**: Track over time, not just point-in-time
5. **No Segmentation**: Break down by team, segment, source
6. **Siloed Metrics**: Align sales, marketing, CS metrics
7. **No Action**: Metrics without action plans are useless

---

## Best Practices

1. **Start Simple**: Begin with 5-7 core metrics
2. **Review Cadence**: Daily for activities, weekly for pipeline, monthly for performance
3. **Trend Analysis**: Track changes over time
4. **Benchmarking**: Compare to industry standards
5. **Segmentation**: Break down by team, product, segment
6. **Visualization**: Dashboards > spreadsheets
7. **Actionability**: Every metric should drive a decision
8. **Alignment**: Ensure sales, marketing, CS use consistent definitions
